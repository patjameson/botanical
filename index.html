Project Statement

The Buffalo Botanical Gardens is in need of a technical document detailing troubleshooting methods in a user friendly and intuitive format. The document must encompass of a variety of current problems, as well as future problems, for the staff at the Botanical Gardens to handle as they arise. This includes issues with the database, the website maintenance, and general electronic glitches that have slowly developed over time. This document will allow for the Botanical Gardens to maintain independence in the future of their current Software Engineer, and no longer need to rely on outside consultants in order to solve problems ranging from intricate solutions to simple fixes. This document will be user friendly so all readers, no matter their technical background or experience, will be able to follow along seamlessly to produce the necessary fixes.

Project Requirements

Introduction of Problem Statement

The Buffalo Botanical Gardens is home to tens of thousands of plant species and information, being visited on a day-to-day basis by students, scholars, and the general public at large. This database requires a large investment in cloud based data, particularly for the entries themselves as well as all of the picture information as well. With such a large scale operation, problems and inefficiencies arise that require outside consultants to resolve. The production of a user friendly troubleshooting guide for the entire staff of the Botanical Gardens to utilize, leading to independence from these consultants, is needed to ensure longevity and customization for the future.

Background Research

The Botanical Gardens is a local operation with a very complex database of tens of thousands of plant species, fauna, and images that is used on a daily basis. This database naturally faces occasional technical glitches or suffers from unintuitive settings, which is currently outsourced to the Botanical Gardenâ€™s Software Engineer for the solution to be devised. The Botanical Garden wishes for a technical document to overcome this hurdle, allowing independence for both parties mutually.

In the event of the Software Engineer being preoccupied with other projects, the Garden could potentially be left with a major technical problem unable to be resolved in the necessary time frame. Further, the Garden, with a textual user manual, will be able to circumvent the inquiry completely, allowing for the Botanical Garden to create solutions for themselves as well as ensuring future customization. The staff needs the user manual to be intuitive such that any member of the staff will be able to access the document and follow along accordingly, in a step by step process to fix the issues as they arise. Creating a document encompassing enough of the multitude of current and potential issues is the challenge to be faced, requiring adaptability and the ability to continuously update the document as issues arise.

Request of the Client

The user manual must be able to be understood by all employees, even those lacking technical skills in the issues at hand. Employees must be able to locate the section where their issue is likely to be, and then determine which troubleshooting method will be best for their particular problem. The processes must follow a simple step-by-step procedure in order to ensure ease of use for the staff members in troubleshooting, yet complex enough to ensure the solution is properly implemented.

Development Design

Due to the balance necessary between technical troubleshooting as well as ease-of-use, the user manual will require a format allowing for easy searching and readability. As such, it will be divided across numerous sections, each denoted within a table of contents, to help direct the user where the most appropriate section for their solution will be. Beyond all else, it must use enough key words to allow for easy access using a search function. Within each section, the detailed methodology must be accessible for the user, yet able to fix the problem at hand with proper diligence. The document must allow for future editing, allowing for updates as issues arise and technology evolves. Further, the document must address current and likely future issues that could develop within the database, and extend beyond the database to the website functionality as well as other software currently utilized by the Gardens.
